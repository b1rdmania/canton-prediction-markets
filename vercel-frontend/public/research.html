KEY RESEARCH PAPERS.

Berg & Rietz (2018) - Longshots and Overconfidence

Seminal work on prediction market biases. Shows that longshots (low-probability events) are systematically overpriced due to behavioral biases. Our mean reversion models exploit this inefficiency.

How It Works

RESEARCH POWERS CANTON AI.

Quality Scoring Algorithm

  • Liquidity Score (0-35): Based on 24h volume
  • Spread Score (0-25): Bid-ask spread tightness
  • Activity Score (0-25): Number of trades and participants
  • Time Score (0-15): Time to resolution

Mean Reversion Detection

  • Historical Averages: Compare to similar past events
  • Fundamental Estimates: Base rate probability analysis
  • Cross-Market Checks: Consistency across related markets

Behavioral Bias Detection

  • Longshot Bias: Overpricing of unlikely events
  • Recency Bias: Overweighting recent news
  • Confirmation Bias: Selective information processing

Why This Matters

  • Credibility: Algorithms tested on real markets
  • Validation: Quality scores identify opportunities
  • Data-Driven: AI grounded in quantitative analysis
  • Transparent: Open-source and verifiable
