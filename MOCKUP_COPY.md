# Mock-up Copy for Canton Weekly Market

## Purpose
Capture the messaging and content we want on the mock front page without prescribing styles or layouts. It is focused on the rhythm of the weekly market, the ledger signals we want to surface, and the human tone (participants, stakes, pool, resolution).

## Section copy

- **Hero**
  - Title: “Canton Market of the Week”
  - Subtitle: “One question. One pool. On Canton.”
  - Question placeholder: “[Headline question goes here]”
  - Countdown label: “Resolves in: [time remaining]”
  - Supporting note: “Deliberately minimal. Built to show real on-ledger flow.”

- **Market Mood (pool split)**
  - Label: “Market mood”
  - Subtext: “Live pool balance”
  - Percentages: “Yes 62% · No 38%”

- **Stake module**
  - Label: “Stake in this pool”
  - Toggle/button text: “Yes” | “No”
  - Amount field placeholder: “Amount (USDC.x)”
  - Optional note field: “Why you’re staking (optional)”
  - CTA: “Stake now”
  - Confirmation text: “Stake recorded.”

- **Participants snapshot**
  - Header text: “42 participants · Pool size: 12,340 USDC.x”
  - Top stakes list entries: e.g., “a84f… · 1,200 USDC.x · Yes”

- **Resolution transparency**
  - Heading: “Resolution”
  - Content: “Designated Resolver · Source: Public announcement”
  - Time note: “Resolution at: 14:00 UTC”

- **Timeline (current highlight)**
  - Steps text: “Curate → Vote-to-list → Trade window → Resolution → Recap”
  - Current phase annotation: “Trade window”

- **Post-resolution recap card**
  - Title: “Resolution recap”
  - Fields:
    * Market: “[question text]”
    * Outcome: “[Yes/No]”
    * Pool size: “[final amount]”
    * Payout: “Completed on Canton”
    * Timestamp: “Resolved at [time]”
  - Footer line: “Resolved on Canton · DAML execution complete”

- **Upcoming vote teaser**
  - Heading: “Vote on next week’s market”
  - Candidate list entries with “View & vote” (3–5)

- **Footer note**
  - Text: “Built for Canton. One market at a time.”

## Tone reminders

- Use “participants”, “stake”, “pool”, “resolution”.
- Avoid “users”, “bets”, “trading”.
- Keep language concise, calm, confident.
